Born in a small town called Nanded in Maharashtra, India, I have been fortunate enough to move to and live in 4 different countries. I am a software engineer by profession and an avid traveler. I started this blog to keep up to date a timeline of my adventures and share my learnings and opinions(subject to change) as I try to optimize my journey.

I spent most of my childhood in the middle east in UAE and moved to India and then the US for my undergraduate studies. I got a degree in Computer Engineering from Purdue University in 2016 and then moved to Seattle to work on autopilots for helicopters at an awesome company called HeliTrak. Eventually, I moved to their parent company Digital Control to work on building underground navigation systems for the drilling industry. Both these places offered me mentorships that have had a significant impact on my life.

In mid-2019, I moved to New York city for Palantir to work on big data problems for a range of clients in industries spanning from finance, cyber-security, to healthcare. During the pandemic of our lifetime, I ended up making a move to London for Palantir to work on some the current healthcare crisis.

Personally, I'm motivated to solve problems in healthcare using technology and identify as a neuro-science enthusiast. My views are neither pure socialist nor capitalist and I love to debate politics. (Ofcourse, in a civil manner!)
