Travel Diary

I plan to walk through some interesting adventures in the different cities / towns I've lived in. This is completely unstructured and for my friends and family to consume. It is not meant for travel enthusiasts looking to get tips on what to see.

London Diaries - Part I

It took me a good 6 months and an embarassing walk back home with a date to realize I know little about the city that I live in. She was sweet enough to tour guide and tell me the origins of Londinium and the distinction between the City of London and Westminster.

That experience left in me an unsatiated need to explore a little more on how London came about. So, I bought this book by Simon Jenkins called "A Short History of London". Simon starts the first chapter encouraging me to ride a bicycle from the Tower of London to Covent Garden... (More)

Guggenheim Musings

One of my cognitive limitations is the inability to appreciate artistic work. I find it especially hard to indulge in second order thinking of any art form that I see. I try to over-index on quantitative attributes on anything that I see. I decided to actively work on that by visiting the Guggenheim Mueseum in New York. It is an art lover's paradise. (More)

The Big Apple - Part I

I was fascinated with the idea of NYC ever since I landed in the US. With contending academic priorities, going to New York never ended up on top of the list. But then, in the summer of 2015, I got an internship at a dimmer manufacturing company called Lutron Electronics. It was in Allentown, Pennsylvania (very unhappening but great breweries!). The key thing though, was that it was just a few hours away from NYC. In fact, to get to Allentown, I took a flight to NYC and drove from JFK, through the disorderly streets of Manhattan to Allentown. I was in awe and this felt like a whole new America (more relatable!). It just felt as if a misfit in general social situations like me would somehow find a place here.
