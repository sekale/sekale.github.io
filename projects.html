Glass


Team mates: Abhishek Srikanth, Kanishk Yadav, Siddhant Ekale
If you don't like the Remember the Name back-drop by Fort Minor, its time to change the URL!

A successful attempt to recreate a cheaper version of existing market products that show information on a heads up display. This project was designed to display time, weather and news notifications as updated on an Android phone. The update events on the phone were subscribed by a Bluetooth Low Energy module(Microchip RN4020) hosted on an M0 Atmel SAMD microcontroller which together were a part of the heads up display's core functionality. This entire product along with the BLE, microcontroller all of which were hosted on a custom PCB costs around 25$ a piece and that is with respect to low production quantity.

Moodify


Team mates: Harika Thatukuru, Namrata Madan, Apoorv Wairagade, Siddhant Ekale

Your very own personal music assistant was made with the noble purpose of helping you cheer up or continue your mood of relaxation. It uses a machine learning library to detect your emotions and curates a spotify playlist off your existing liked songs. Spotify music items have a property called valence which measures how up-beat the song is. If your mood is hyper-excited, it tries to calm you down, if it's low, then it'll select up-beat songs.

Infiniti


Team mates: Abhishek Srikanth, Pranav Punjabi, Siddhant Ekale

This was a HACK UIUC project where we interfaced a gyroscope to an intel linux based system on chip to be able to control movement of bikes in a custom made racing game. The game that we built was inspired from RoadRash (if you didn't play this when you were a kid, you're not a 90's kid!), except that it was purely built using HTML5 and Javascript.
